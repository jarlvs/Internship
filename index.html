A generic drug is a medication created to be the same as an already marketed brand-name drug in dosage form, safety, strength, route of administration, quality, performance characteristics, and intended use. These similarities help to demonstrate bioequivalence, which means that a generic medicine works in the same way and provides the same clinical benefit as its brand-name version. In other words, you can take a generic medicine as an equal substitute for its brand-name counterpart.

Generico is the fastest growing specialized pharmacy retail chain of Generic medicines. Started by IIT Bombay alumni, Generico aims at making healthcare affordable for millions of Indians. FAQs

What is generic?
Technically, any drug whose patent period is over, falls into Generic category. Post the patent period many pharma companies can make the same drug and the price falls significantly.
Are the medicines prescribed by doctor also generic?
In India 85% of prescribed medicines are Generics. However due to immense marketing by the pharma companies, the price benefit does not reach the patients and even the generic medicines are costing much higher.
Do all medicines come in generics?
Yes, Majority of the medicines falls into generic category in India. In fact, India is the world’s biggest supplier of generic medicines.
Does it have same effect as branded?
Yes, Generics have same effect, power and strength as their branded counterparts.
Why should I take it from Generico and not any other store?
GENERICO team has a mission to serve our customers with the highest quality medicines at the most economical prices. We are the most trusted and fastest growing specialized pharmacy chain having served generics to more than hundred thousand customers. We do not compromise on quality and have our internal checks and process to ensure you get the highest quality generics from India’s top pharma companies. The generics from local retailers may not be as high quality as from GENERICO.
Why it is priced cheaper than branded?
Since the generic medicines are not being marketed, they end at lesser rates to Retail and thus lot of savings can be passed to patients. When generic medicines start getting marketed (like the case in India) they become expensive. For instance, if Pharma Company decide to stop marketing their Prescribed Generics, they will also become cheaper for patients.
Do I need a generic prescription to get generic medicines?
Lot of doctors now write generic name of medicines also. In case it is not written, Generico has qualified pharmacists to suggest you the generic alternatives which you can refer to doctor before consumption.
Is it necessary to show it to Doctor?
You should refer to doctor before consuming any medicines. Generico encourages patients to refer the medicines to doctors before consumption
